1 And he went out from thence, and came into his own country; and his disciples follow him.1 At umalis siya doon; at napasa kaniyang sariling lupain; at nagsisunod sa kaniya ang kaniyang mga alagad.
2 And when the sabbath day was come, he began to teach in the synagogue: and many hearing him were astonished, saying, From whence hath this man these things? and what wisdom is this which is given unto him, that even such mighty works are wrought by his hands?2 At nang dumating ang sabbath, ay nagpasimulang magturo siya sa sinagoga: at marami sa nangakakarinig sa kaniya ay nangagtataka, na nangagsasabi, Saan nagkaroon ang taong ito ng mga bagay na ito? at, Anong karunungan ito na sa kaniya'y ibinigay, at anong kahulugan ng gayong mga makapangyarihang gawa na ginagawa ng kaniyang mga kamay?
3 Is not this the carpenter, the son of Mary, the brother of James, and Joses, and of Juda, and Simon? and are not his sisters here with us? And they were offended at him.3 Hindi baga ito ang anluwagi, ang anak ni Maria, at kapatid ni Santiago, at ni Jose, at ni Judas, at ni Simon? at hindi baga nangaririto sa atin ang kaniyang mga kapatid na babae? At siya'y kinatitisuran nila.
4 But Jesus said unto them, A prophet is not without honour, but in his own country, and among his own kin, and in his own house.4 At sinabi sa kanila ni Jesus, Walang propetang di may kapurihan, liban sa kaniyang sariling lupain, at sa gitna ng kaniyang sariling mga kamaganak, at sa kaniyang sariling bahay.
5 And he could there do no mighty work, save that he laid his hands upon a few sick folk, and healed them.5 At hindi siya nakagawa doon ng anomang makapangyarihang gawa, liban sa ipinatong niya ang kaniyang mga kamay sa ilang mga maysakit, at pinagaling sila.
6 And he marvelled because of their unbelief. And he went round about the villages, teaching.6 At nanggigilalas siya sa kanilang di pananampalataya. At siya'y lumibot na nagtuturo sa mga nayong nasa paligidligid.
7 And he called unto him the twelve, and began to send them forth by two and two; and gave them power over unclean spirits;7 At pinalapit niya sa kaniya ang labingdalawa, at nagpasimulang sinugo sila na daladalawa; at binigyan niya sila ng kapamahalaan laban sa mga karumaldumal na espiritu;
8 And commanded them that they should take nothing for their journey, save a staff only; no scrip, no bread, no money in their purse:8 At ipinagbilin niya sa kanila na huwag silang magsipagbaon ng anoman sa paglakad, kundi tungkod lamang; kahit tinapay, kahit supot ng ulam, kahit salapi sa kanilang supot;
9 But be shod with sandals; and not put on two coats.9 Datapuwa't gumamit ng mga sandalyas: at, huwag magsuot ng dalawang tunika.
10 And he said unto them, In what place soever ye enter into an house, there abide till ye depart from that place.10 At sinabi niya sa kanila, Saan man kayo magsipasok sa isang bahay, mangatira kayo roon hanggang sa kayo'y magsialis doon.
11 And whosoever shall not receive you, nor hear you, when ye depart thence, shake off the dust under your feet for a testimony against them. Verily I say unto you, It shall be more tolerable for Sodom and Gomorrha in the day of judgment, than for that city.11 At sa alin mang dakong hindi kayo tanggapin, at hindi kayo pakinggan, pagalis ninyo doo'y ipagpag ninyo ang alabok na nasa ilalim ng inyong talampakan bilang patotoo sa kanila.
12 And they went out, and preached that men should repent.12 At sila'y nangagsialis, at nagsipangaral na mangagsisi ang mga tao.
13 And they cast out many devils, and anointed with oil many that were sick, and healed them.13 At nangagpalabas ng maraming demonio, at nangagpahid ng langis sa maraming may-sakit, at pinagaling sila.
14 And king Herod heard of him; (for his name was spread abroad:) and he said, That John the Baptist was risen from the dead, and therefore mighty works do shew forth themselves in him.14 At narinig ng haring Herodes; sapagka't nabantog na ang pangalan niya; at sinabi niya, Si Juan na Mangbabautismo ay nagbangon sa mga patay, at kaya sumasa kaniya ang mga kapangyarihang ito.
15 Others said, That it is Elias. And others said, That it is a prophet, or as one of the prophets.15 At sinasabi ng mga iba, Siya'y si Elias. At sinasabi ng mga iba, Siya'y propeta, na gaya ng ibang mga propeta.
16 But when Herod heard thereof, he said, It is John, whom I beheaded: he is risen from the dead.16 Datapuwa't nang marinig ni Herodes, ay sinabi, Si Juan na aking pinugutan ng ulo, siya'y nagbangon.
17 For Herod himself had sent forth and laid hold upon John, and bound him in prison for Herodias' sake, his brother Philip's wife: for he had married her.17 Sapagka't si Herodes din ang nagsugo sa mga kawal at nagpahuli kay Juan, at nagpatanikala sa kaniya sa bilangguan dahil kay Herodias, na asawa ni Filipo na kaniyang kapatid; sapagka't nagasawa siya sa kaniya.
18 For John had said unto Herod, It is not lawful for thee to have thy brother's wife.18 Sapagka't sinabi ni Juan kay Herodes, Hindi matuwid sa iyo na iyong ariin ang asawa ng iyong kapatid.
19 Therefore Herodias had a quarrel against him, and would have killed him; but she could not:19 At ipinagtanim siya ni Herodias, at hinahangad siyang patayin; at hindi niya magawa;
20 For Herod feared John, knowing that he was a just man and an holy, and observed him; and when he heard him, he did many things, and heard him gladly.20 Sapagka't natatakot si Herodes kay Juan palibhasa'y nalalamang siya'y lalaking matuwid at banal, at siya'y ipinagsanggalang niya. At kung siya'y pinakikinggan niya, ay natitilihan siyang mainam; at pinakikinggan niya siya na may galak.
21 And when a convenient day was come, that Herod on his birthday made a supper to his lords, high captains, and chief estates of Galilee;21 At nang sumapit ang isang kaukulang araw, na kapanganakan niya, ay ipinaghanda ni Herodes ng isang hapunan ang kaniyang mga maginoo, at mga mataas na kapitan, at mga pangulong lalake sa Galilea;
22 And when the daughter of the said Herodias came in, and danced, and pleased Herod and them that sat with him, the king said unto the damsel, Ask of me whatsoever thou wilt, and I will give it thee.22 At nang pumasok ang anak na babae ni Herodias ay sumayaw, at siya'y kinalugdan ni Herodes at ng mga kasalo niyang nakaupo sa dulang; at sinabi ng hari sa dalaga, Hingin mo sa akin ang maibigan mo, at ibibigay ko sa iyo.
23 And he sware unto her, Whatsoever thou shalt ask of me, I will give it thee, unto the half of my kingdom.23 At ipinanumpa niya sa kaniya, Ang anomang hingin mo sa akin ay ibibigay ko sa iyo, kahit ang kalahati ng aking kaharian.
24 And she went forth, and said unto her mother, What shall I ask? And she said, The head of John the Baptist.24 At lumabas siya, at sinabi sa kaniyang ina, Ano ang aking hihingin? At sinabi niya, Ang ulo ni Juan ang Mangbabautismo.
25 And she came in straightway with haste unto the king, and asked, saying, I will that thou give me by and by in a charger the head of John the Baptist.25 At pagdaka'y pumasok siyang dalidali sa kinaroroonan ng hari, at humingi, na sinasabi, Ibig ko na ngayon din ang ibigay mo sa akin na nasa isang pinggan ang ulo ni Juan Bautista.
26 And the king was exceeding sorry; yet for his oath's sake, and for their sakes which sat with him, he would not reject her.26 At namanglaw na lubha ang hari; datapuwa't dahil sa kaniyang sumpa, at sa nangakaupo sa dulang, ay hindi niya itinanggi.
27 And immediately the king sent an executioner, and commanded his head to be brought: and he went and beheaded him in the prison,27 At pagdaka'y nagsugo ang hari sa isang kawal na kaniyang bantay, at ipinagutos na dalhin sa kaniya ang ulo niya: at yumaon siya at pinugutan siya ng ulo sa bilangguan,
28 And brought his head in a charger, and gave it to the damsel: and the damsel gave it to her mother.28 At dinala ang kaniyang ulo na nasa isang pinggan, at ibinigay sa dalaga; at ibinigay ng dalaga sa kaniyang ina.
29 And when his disciples heard of it, they came and took up his corpse, and laid it in a tomb.29 At nang mabalitaan ng kaniyang mga alagad, ay nagsiparoon sila at binuhat ang kaniyang bangkay, at inilagay sa isang libingan.
30 And the apostles gathered themselves together unto Jesus, and told him all things, both what they had done, and what they had taught.30 At ang mga apostol ay nangagpisan kay Jesus; at isinaysay nila sa kaniya ang lahat ng mga bagay na kanilang ginawa, at ang lahat ng kanilang itinuro.
31 And he said unto them, Come ye yourselves apart into a desert place, and rest a while: for there were many coming and going, and they had no leisure so much as to eat.31 At sinabi niya sa kanila, Magsiparito kayo ng bukod sa isang dakong ilang, at mangagpahinga kayo ng kaunti. Sapagka't marami ang nangagpaparoo't parito, at sila'y hindi man lamang mangagkapanahon na magsikain.
32 And they departed into a desert place by ship privately.32 At nagsiyaon silang nangasa daong at nangapasa isang dakong ilang at bukod.
33 And the people saw them departing, and many knew him, and ran afoot thither out of all cities, and outwent them, and came together unto him.33 At nangakita sila ng mga tao sa pagalis, at sila'y nangakilala ng marami at paraparang nagsisitakbo na nagsiparoon doon mula sa lahat ng mga bayan, at nangaunang nagsirating pa kay sa kanila.
34 And Jesus, when he came out, saw much people, and was moved with compassion toward them, because they were as sheep not having a shepherd: and he began to teach them many things.34 At lumabas siya at nakita ang lubhang maraming tao, at nahabag siya sa kanila, sapagka't sila'y gaya ng mga tupa na walang pastor: at siya'y nagpasimulang tinuruan sila ng maraming bagay.
35 And when the day was now far spent, his disciples came unto him, and said, This is a desert place, and now the time is far passed:35 At nang gumabi na, ay nagsilapit sa kaniya ang kaniyang mga alagad, at nangagsabi, Ilang ang dakong ito, at gumagabi na;
36 Send them away, that they may go into the country round about, and into the villages, and buy themselves bread: for they have nothing to eat.36 Payaunin mo sila, upang sila'y magsiparoon sa mga bayan at mga nayon sa palibotlibot nito, at mangagsibili ng anomang makakain.
37 He answered and said unto them, Give ye them to eat. And they say unto him, Shall we go and buy two hundred pennyworth of bread, and give them to eat?37 Datapuwa't siya'y sumagot at sinabi sa kanila, Bigyan ninyo sila ng makakain. At sinabi nila sa kaniya, Magsisiyaon ba kami at magsisibili ng dalawang daang denariong tinapay, at ipakakain namin sa kanila?
38 He saith unto them, How many loaves have ye? go and see. And when they knew, they say, Five, and two fishes.38 At sinabi niya sa kanila, Ilang tinapay mayroon kayo? magsiparoon kayo at inyong tingnan. At nang mangaalaman nila, ay kanilang sinabi, Lima, at dalawang isda.
39 And he commanded them to make all sit down by companies upon the green grass.39 At iniutos niya sa kanila na paupuin silang lahat na pulupulutong sa ibabaw ng damuhang sariwa.
40 And they sat down in ranks, by hundreds, and by fifties.40 At sila'y nagsiupong hanayhanay, na tigsasangdaan, at tiglilimangpu.
41 And when he had taken the five loaves and the two fishes, he looked up to heaven, and blessed, and brake the loaves, and gave them to his disciples to set before them; and the two fishes divided he among them all.41 At kinuha niya ang limang tinapay at ang dalawang isda, at pagtingala sa langit, ay kaniyang pinagpala, at pinagputolputol ang mga tinapay; at ibinigay niya sa mga alagad upang ihain nila sa kanila; at ipinamahagi niya sa kanilang lahat ang dalawang isda.
42 And they did all eat, and were filled.42 At nagsikain silang lahat, at nangabusog.
43 And they took up twelve baskets full of the fragments, and of the fishes.43 At kanilang pinulot ang mga pinagputolputol, labingdalawang bakol na puno ng tinapay at mga isda naman.
44 And they that did eat of the loaves were about five thousand men.44 At ang nagsikain ng mga tinapay ay limang libong lalake.
45 And straightway he constrained his disciples to get into the ship, and to go to the other side before unto Bethsaida, while he sent away the people.45 At pagdaka'y pinalulan niya sa daong ang kaniyang mga alagad, at pinauna sa kaniya sa kabilang ibayo, sa Betsaida, samantalang pinayayaon niya ang karamihan.
46 And when he had sent them away, he departed into a mountain to pray.46 At pagkatapos na mapagpaalam niya sila, ay naparoon siya sa bundok upang manalangin.
47 And when even was come, the ship was in the midst of the sea, and he alone on the land.47 At nang dumating ang gabi, ang daong ay nasa gitna ng dagat, at siya'y nagiisa sa lupa.
48 And he saw them toiling in rowing; for the wind was contrary unto them: and about the fourth watch of the night he cometh unto them, walking upon the sea, and would have passed by them.48 At pagkakita sa kanila na totoong nangalulumbay sa paggaod, sapagka't sinasalunga sila ng hangin, at malapit na ang ikaapat na pagpupuyat sa gabi ay naparoon siya sa kanila, na lumalakad sa ibabaw ng dagat; at ibig silang lagpasan:
49 But when they saw him walking upon the sea, they supposed it had been a spirit, and cried out:49 Datapuwa't sila, nang makita nilang siya'y lumalakad sa ibabaw ng dagat, ay inakala nilang siya'y isang multo, at nangagsisigaw;
50 For they all saw him, and were troubled. And immediately he talked with them, and saith unto them, Be of good cheer: it is I; be not afraid.50 Sapagka't nakita siya nilang lahat, at nangagulumihanan. Datapuwa't pagdaka'y nagsalita siya sa kanila, at sa kanila'y sinabi, Laksan ninyo ang inyong loob: ako nga; huwag kayong mangatakot.
51 And he went up unto them into the ship; and the wind ceased: and they were sore amazed in themselves beyond measure, and wondered.51 At pinanhik niya sila sa daong; at humimpil ang hangin: at sila'y nanganggilalas ng di kawasa sa kanilang sarili;
52 For they considered not the miracle of the loaves: for their heart was hardened.52 Palibhasa'y hindi pa nila natatalastas yaong tungkol sa mga tinapay, dahil sa ang kanilang puso'y pinatigas.
53 And when they had passed over, they came into the land of Gennesaret, and drew to the shore.53 At nang mangakatawid na sila, ay narating nila ang lupa ng Genezaret, at nagsisadsad sa daungan.
54 And when they were come out of the ship, straightway they knew him,54 At paglunsad nila sa daong, pagdaka'y nakilala siya ng mga tao,
55 And ran through that whole region round about, and began to carry about in beds those that were sick, where they heard he was.55 At nang malibot nilang nagtutumulin ang buong lupaing yaon, at nagpasimulang dalhin sa kaniya ang mga may-sakit na nasa kanilang higaan, saan man nila marinig na naroon siya.
56 And whithersoever he entered, into villages, or cities, or country, they laid the sick in the streets, and besought him that they might touch if it were but the border of his garment: and as many as touched him were made whole.56 At saan man siya pumasok, sa mga nayon, o sa mga bayan o sa mga bukid, ay inilalagay nila sa mga liwasan ang mga may-sakit, at ipinamamanhik sa kaniya na ipahipo man lamang sa kanila ang laylayan ng kaniyang damit: at ang lahat ng nagsihipo sa kaniya ay pawang nagsigaling.
